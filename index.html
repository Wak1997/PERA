Punjab Enforcement and Regulation Act 2024 (PERA Act)

Question 1: Where does the second appeal lie after an unsuccessful representation before the Hearing Officer?

Answer:

The second appeal lies before the Court of Session or Additional Sessions Judge.

Explanation:

  • After an unsuccessful representation before the Hearing Officer, the aggrieved party can appeal to the Commissioner of the concerned Division (first appeal). If still aggrieved, they can file a second appeal to the Court of Session or Additional Sessions Judge, as defined in Section 2(i) and governed by Section 52(3) of the PERA Act.
  • Section 52(1): Allows the first appeal to the Commissioner.
  • Section 52(3): Allows the second appeal to the Court (Court of Session or Additional Sessions Judge).
  • The PERA Act’s appeal mechanism takes precedence over other laws, as per Section 77, and no further appeal is provided beyond the Court of Session.

Question 2: Who has the power to register an FIR?

Answer:

The Sub Divisional Enforcement Officer (SDEO) has the power to register an FIR under the PERA Act.

Explanation:

  • Section 22(4)(f): Empowers the Sub Divisional Enforcement Officer to "register FIRs and conduct investigations."
  • Section 49(2): Specifies scenarios where the Sub Divisional Enforcement Officer can register an FIR, including directly or based on reports from other officers.
  • While the Enforcement Officer and Investigation Officer can file complaints, the actual registration of the FIR is performed by the Sub Divisional Enforcement Officer.
  • The Enforcement Station, not a police station, is the designated entity for FIR registration under the Act, as per Section 19.

Question 3: Who has the authority to arrest a person?

Answer:

The following officers have the authority to arrest a person under the PERA Act:

  • Sub Divisional Enforcement Officer (SDEO)
  • Enforcement Officer (EO)
  • Investigation Officer (IO)

Explanation:

  • Section 31(1): Explicitly grants arrest powers to these officers under specific conditions, such as involvement in an offence or obstruction of duties.
  • Supporting sections:
    • Section 22(4)(f): Confirms the Sub Divisional Enforcement Officer (SDEO) arrest authority.
    • Section 23(4)(viii): Grants the Enforcement Officer (EO) arrest powers.
    • Section 26(3)(f): Confirms the Investigation Officer (IO) arrest powers.
  • Section 32: Requires the arresting officer to present the person at the Enforcement Station and produce them before a Magistrate within 24 hours.
  • The CrPC’s arrest procedures apply where not inconsistent with the PERA Act, as per Section 33.
